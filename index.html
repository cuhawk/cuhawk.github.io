Cyber Security (March 2021 - June 2022)

Started from TryHackMe, with the programming and basic web development and handling servers on AWS and GCP. Learnt the basics usage of tools and concepts like SQLi, XSS, CSRF, Basic Enumeration, Metasploit, Web Attacks, Burpsuite and other tools and techniques. Later switched to HackTheBox. The Easy, Medium, Hard and Insane Boxes took the difficulty to literally another level. Youtuber IppSec, John Hammond and 0xdf taught a lot.

After a couple of months, I decided to take OSCP course from Offensive Security. They had changed the syllabus in 2020, so the content was very new, equally difficult too. Completed 70+ machines from the lab after the PDF and Videos. Took me 3 months for the lab only. Now since I clearly needed more experience, I switched back to doing the old machines on HackTheBox for a couple of months. Then gave the OSCP Exam. Completed 70 marks of machines which was exactly what was expected. But I never concentrated at Documentation, so I was declared Failed. I again rebounded to HaackTheBox, did a few more boxes.

It had been over six months since I got involved in Cyber Security. I now took the OSEP course since OSCP is a beginner level course I wanted to try something really difficult and challenging. This time I was careful with Notes and Documentation. Completed The PDF and videos, Later the 6 labs also. Which were mainly focussed on Active Directory Enumeration. The labs itself were fun. I was able to complete all 6 challenged within OSEP lab in 3 months. I was moved to give the exam but from OSCP exam experience, i needed more experience. I wanted to get the TRY HARDER mentality as said by Offensive Security peeps. So from the Discord channel I got suggested to HackTheBox ProLabs Cybernetics. In all the machines and challenges i had ever done this was the most difficult one. It had 25 machines, each were extremely difficult. I have planned to complete the OSEP exam in September/October 2022.

Quadcopter

Initially this project was started with little hope as this was my first robotics project i took on my own. So learning was a little difficult. Based on only arduino to barely optimize and control the PIDs of the quadcopter, which suceeded the 6th time.

After and year, i thought to upgrade by making it Autonomous. Throwing in Raspberry pi and other sensors, this time doing it right, with Robot Operating System(ROS) and GAZEBO. Simulizing and running in python and c++ on raspberry pi then the right values simulated were to be pushed to the connected arduino then to the feedback of brushless motors.

After completion of this project i got to publish this in Vedanth as a junior.Later that year, I became the first person to present it at the Vedanth Tech tatva event. previous year tyo the strider publishing. Then with a couple of peeps I though to take it to next level, Created the CAD model for an Agricultural Drone with all types of analysis.But could not get the required funding to initiate a mini startup.
